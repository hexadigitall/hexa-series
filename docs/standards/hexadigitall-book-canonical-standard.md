# Hexadigitall Book Canonical Standard

Version: 1.0  
Owner: Hexadigitall Curriculum and Product Team  
Last Updated: 2026-04-22

## Purpose

This standard defines how every Hexadigitall book is designed, written, reviewed, produced, and maintained.

It exists to guarantee that each title:

- matches the HexaVerse narrative and educational vision
- is age-appropriate and scientifically accurate
- is structurally consistent across digital and print formats
- can be sold, taught, and extended without rework

## Scope

This applies to all book-format products in the Hexa-AI-Series ecosystem:

- story-led books for young learners
- technical narrative books for teens and advanced learners
- companion textbooks and guided workbooks

This standard does not replace format-specific rules for courses, labs, or capstones. It defines the canonical baseline for books.

## Audience Bands and Learning Promise

Audience bands:

- Ages 5-9: story-first, tactile learning, short language loops
- Ages 10-14: challenge-first, engineering reasoning, guided systems thinking
- Ages 15+: model-first, technical depth, ethics and tradeoff framing
- Advanced: architecture-first, implementation and operations rigor

Learning promise per title:

- I can explain the core idea in my own words.
- I can apply the idea in an activity, simulation, or challenge.
- I can connect this idea to previous and next HexaVerse concepts.

## Canonical Content Structure

Each book must include all sections below in this order.

1. Cover and identity
2. Reader promise and intended outcomes
3. Story or concept opener
4. Core concept modules
5. Interaction points
6. Check-for-understanding section
7. Glossary
8. Real-world connection
9. Cross-links to adjacent titles
10. Adult guide (teacher or parent)

### Required chapter blocks

Each chapter must include:

- Hook: why this matters
- Explain: concept in age-appropriate language
- Show: visual, metaphor, or simulation
- Try: mini activity
- Reflect: one prompt
- Check: one formative question

## Learning and Pedagogy Rules

- One primary concept per chapter. Secondary concepts are clearly marked.
- New terms are introduced once, then reinforced in context.
- Cognitive load is controlled: short sections, predictable rhythm, progressive depth.
- Every major claim is either demonstrated, modeled, or referenced in glossary notes.
- Misconception handling is explicit: one "common confusion" note per chapter when needed.

## Science Accuracy Rules

- No unsupported absolute claims in physics, biology, AI, or space systems.
- Distinguish clearly between current capability, prototype capability, and speculative future.
- Include one evidence check pass before approval.
- Include one date marker for time-sensitive facts.

### Claim taxonomy

Use one of these labels for technical statements:

- Established: broadly validated and accepted
- Emerging: under active research or early deployment
- Speculative: plausible but not yet validated

## Narrative and Voice Rules

- HexaVerse tone: wonder plus systems clarity
- Avoid fear-based framing; frame hard problems as design challenges
- Keep metaphor accuracy high; avoid metaphors that teach incorrect physics
- Use consistent character canon for Hexa and world references

## Design and Interaction Rules

Digital and print must feel like one product family.

Required design behaviors:

- clear visual hierarchy
- arc color mapping consistency
- consistent iconography for glossary, activity, challenge, and safety notes
- accessibility baseline across formats

Interaction minimum per title:

- at least 3 interactive checkpoints
- at least 1 applied challenge
- at least 1 reflection prompt

## Illustration and Visual Standards

Every book must include a robust visual language that works across print, digital, and merchandise.

### Art style requirements

- **Visual identity:** illustrations match HexaVerse character design and world aesthetic
- **Consistency:** same character appears the same across all titles
- **Technical accuracy:** diagrams and scientific illustrations are reviewed by domain expert
- **Accessibility:** color schemes pass WCAG AA contrast standards; diagrams have text alternatives
- **Production quality:** minimum 300 DPI for print; vector formats for logos and icons

### Illustration content requirements

Minimum illustration count per book:

- One cover illustration (full-bleed, print-safe)
- One illustration per major concept section
- One diagram per technical or spatial concept
- One character-driven scene per chapter (for Ages 5-14)
- One callout illustration per glossary term (optional but encouraged)

### Character canon and world consistency

- **Hexa the protagonist:** depicted consistently across all titles, aging with the arc (younger in Galactic Playground, mature in Silicon Astronaut)
- **Secondary characters:** recurring supporting characters (mentors, peer learners, domain experts) must be reused across titles to build familiarity
- **World environments:** HexaVerse planets, habitats, and spaces illustrated consistently
- **Tech and objects:** spacecraft, rovers, habitats, lab equipment illustrated with technical plausibility

### Illustration licensing and rights

- All original illustrations must be work-for-hire or fully licensed to Hexadigitall
- Photographer/illustrator credits must appear in book back matter
- Any third-party imagery must have explicit usage rights for print and digital formats
- IP ownership must be registered as Hexadigitall trademark asset

## Accessibility and Inclusion Baseline

- Age-appropriate language without gatekeeping jargon
- Inclusive examples and character framing
- High-contrast color choices for text and key diagrams
- Alt text for digital images and diagram captions
- Print-safe typography and spacing

## Gamified Learning Mechanisms

Every book includes at least one interactive gamified challenge that reinforces vocabulary, concept mastery, and creative synthesis. These are Hexadigitall signature features.

### Feature 1: Alphabet Explorer (A-Z Challenge)

**Branding:** "Alphabet Explorer" or "A-Z Quest"

**Mechanism:** For each major concept, learners supply one word starting with each letter A-Z that relates to the topic.

Example for gravity: Attraction, Bodies (celestial), Center (of mass)... Zero-G

**Implementation:**
- Interactive fillable form (digital) or worksheet (print)
- Suggestions list available as hint scaffold for younger learners
- Solution checker highlights correct answers
- Difficulty levels: assisted (with word bank), guided (with hints), free-form (no aids)

**Learning outcome:** learners demonstrate vocabulary mastery and concept association

### Feature 2: Ten-Word Master Challenge

**Branding:** "Ten-Word Master" or "Alphabet Mastery"

**Mechanism:** For a specific letter, learners find or recall 10 words from the chapter that start with that letter.

**Implementation:**
- Prompted challenge: "Find 10 words starting with [letter] from this chapter"
- Digital: autocomplete and validation
- Print: answer key in teacher guide
- Difficulty levels: chapter-only words, chapter + glossary, free-form knowledge

**Learning outcome:** deep review of chapter vocabulary, pattern recognition, lexical expansion

### Feature 3: Concept Remix (Mashup Challenge)

**Branding:** "Concept Mashup" or "Remix Challenge"

**Mechanism:** Learners combine ideas from multiple chapters into a new scenario or invention.

Example: "Use one concept from the chapter on gravity and one from sound to design a new communication system."

**Implementation:**
- Open-ended creative prompt
- Rubric for evaluation (digital feedback, teacher guide)
- Gallery view for sharing student work

**Learning outcome:** transfer learning, systems thinking, creative application

### Gamification design standards

- **Progression:** challenges scale in difficulty across chapters
- **Feedback:** immediate positive reinforcement for correct answers
- **Optional mechanics:** optional badges, streaks, or achievement tracking (age-appropriate)
- **Inclusivity:** all challenges have no-penalty exploration mode
- **Print parity:** every digital challenge has a printable equivalent

## Production Assets Checklist

A book is not production-ready unless all required assets exist.

Required files:

- story manuscript
- chapter outline
- glossary source
- interaction spec
- artwork list and rights status
- illustration files (minimum 300 DPI for print)
- metadata file
- gamification challenge specifications
- review log
- release notes

## Review and Approval Gates

Gate 1: Content completeness

- all required sections present
- metadata complete
- chapter objectives mapped
- all illustrations received and rights verified

Gate 2: Science and pedagogy

- science reviewer approved
- pedagogy reviewer approved
- misconceptions addressed
- gamification challenges validated for learning outcome

Gate 3: Product readiness

- design QA passed (visual consistency, character canon, arc color)
- accessibility QA passed (WCAG AA, color contrast, alt text)
- print constraints validated if print edition exists
- illustration DPI and format validated for print
- trademark and branding elements verified

Gate 4: IP and legal

- all illustration rights cleared
- trademark registrations confirmed or initiated
- patent applications filed if applicable
- all contributor/illustrator agreements signed

Gate 5: Launch

- final sign-off by product owner
- ISBN workflow initiated if applicable
- catalog and sales copy prepared
- quality assurance badges earned and displayed

## Versioning and Editions

Edition model:

- Major edition for structural or curriculum shifts
- Minor edition for chapter-level enhancements
- Patch edition for typo, errata, and small corrections

Version format:

- Major.Minor.Patch

Every edition must have:

- release notes
- updated metadata
- changelog summary

## IP and Patent Considerations

The gamified mechanisms, character canon, and visual language represent valuable intellectual property.

### Patentable elements

Consider patent applications for:

- **Alphabet Explorer** interactive algorithm and mechanics (educational gamification with vocabulary scaffolding)
- **Ten-Word Challenge** methodology for distributed vocabulary mastery
- **Concept Remix** framework for transfer learning assessment
- Visual character recognition systems (if AI-based grading is implemented)

### Trademark and design assets

Register as Hexadigitall trademarks:

- "Alphabet Explorer™" name and logo
- "Ten-Word Master™" name and logo
- HexaVerse character design and world aesthetic
- Color palette and design system (arc-specific colors)
- Iconic elements (Hexa silhouette, planetary symbols, etc.)

### IP strategy

- File provisional patents for gamification mechanics before public release
- Register trademark for feature names and logos before marketing campaigns
- License character designs only to vetted partners
- Include IP ownership clauses in all contributor and contractor agreements
- Monitor for unauthorized use of trademarked features in educational tech space

## Hexadigitall Trademark Standards

Every book is a carrier of Hexadigitall brand identity and educational philosophy.

### Brand promise

*"From Wonder to Wisdom: Learning the Frontier."*

Every book reinforces:

- **Wonder:** science and space are awe-inspiring and accessible
- **Systems:** complex problems have multiple solutions; engineering is creative
- **Agency:** young learners can think like builders and designers
- **Diversity:** protagonists and examples reflect multiple backgrounds and perspectives

### Visual brand markers

- Hexadigitall logo appears on cover, spine, and inside front matter
- Arc color bar on spine (Galactic Playground: purple, Mission to Mars: orange, Silicon Astronaut: cyan, Automating Galaxy: green)
- Hexa character appears on cover and chapter openers as visual anchor
- QR code or URL linking to HexaVerse digital companion

### Language and tone markers

- Each chapter opens with a hook that connects to real-world engineering or science challenge
- Challenges are framed as "design problems" not "exercises"
- Mistakes are "debugging moments" not "wrong answers"
- Learners are addressed as "Explorers," "Engineers," or "Builders" depending on age band

### Quality markers

- "Hexadigitall Approved™" seal for titles meeting all standards
- "Science Reviewed™" badge for titles with expert review
- "Educator Tested™" badge for titles piloted in classrooms
- "Accessibility Certified™" badge for titles meeting WCAG AA standard

### Cross-promotion and ecosystem branding

- Each book includes "Next in the series" preview
- Gamified challenges include easter eggs linking to adjacent titles
- QR codes in print edition link to supplementary digital content
- Teacher guides include connection suggestions to companion courses and labs

## Book Readiness Scorecard

Use this simple score before publication.

Scoring dimensions:

- Concept clarity (0-5)
- Scientific integrity (0-5)
- Age fit (0-5)
- Narrative strength (0-5)
- Interaction quality (0-5)
- Accessibility (0-5)
- Production completeness (0-5)

Publishing threshold:

- minimum total 28/35
- no category below 3/5

## Non-Negotiables

- No title ships without complete metadata.
- No technical claim ships without taxonomy label and review.
- No book ships without at least one applied challenge.
- No print edition ships without print QA sign-off.
