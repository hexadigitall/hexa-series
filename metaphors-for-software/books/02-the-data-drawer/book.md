### The Story of the Archivist Who Lost Everything

> [Draft pending — story should open in a chaotic archive room. A newly appointed archivist inherits a predecessor's desk: papers everywhere, no labels, no system. The archivist's first crisis: a document is urgently needed and cannot be found. Resolution comes through designing a naming and sorting system — specific containers for specific kinds of things. The turning point: the archivist realizes that not all documents are the same *kind* of thing. Some are names (text). Some are dates (numbers with format). Some are lists. Some are maps of name-to-content. The system must account for type before it can function.]
