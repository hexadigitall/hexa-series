# Illustrator Job Description

**Role:** Children's Book Illustrator, HexaVerse Series

**Company:** Hexadigitall Technologies

**Location:** Remote

**Engagement Type:** Contract, project-based with long-term renewal potential

**Project Window:** Initial 12-24 month collaboration

---

## Role Summary

Hexadigitall is hiring a children's book illustrator to define and scale the visual world of the HexaVerse, an expanding educational universe that teaches science, space, AI, systems thinking, and future civilization design.

This is not a one-off illustration assignment. We are looking for a partner who can help establish a durable, recognizable series identity across multiple books and age bands.

The first scope centers on the Galactic Playground arc, beginning with:
- Why Does the Apple Fall?
- Sound and the Singing Air
- Light is Always in a Hurry
- The Gravity Lab
- My Robot Friend

You will help bring Hexa, the series protagonist, to life across a progression from early-childhood wonder to advanced frontier systems thinking.

---

## What You Will Do

- Illustrate covers and interior scenes for children's educational books
- Translate scientific concepts into clear, engaging visual storytelling
- Develop and maintain consistent character design across multiple titles
- Work from detailed story manuscripts, character briefs, and art direction guides
- Produce publication-ready assets for both print and digital formats
- Collaborate with curriculum and publishing leads through structured review cycles
- Contribute to a repeatable illustration system that can scale across the series

---

## What We Need From You

### Required
- Strong portfolio in children's illustration, editorial illustration, or educational publishing
- Ability to maintain style consistency across a multi-book series
- Strong character design skills, including aging a recurring protagonist across arcs
- Comfort illustrating scientific, technical, and speculative themes without losing warmth or clarity
- Experience delivering layered source files and print-ready assets
- Ability to work from briefs, take direction, and iterate reliably
- Strong communication and timeline discipline

### Preferred
- Previous children's STEM publishing experience
- Experience with inclusive and accessible illustration practices
- Experience designing recurring cast and world systems
- Comfort with educational content that spans ages 5 through teens
- Familiarity with print production standards such as bleed, CMYK conversion, and 300 DPI workflows

---

## Visual Direction

We are not looking for generic classroom clip art, flat stock illustration, or ultra-minimal corporate visuals.

We are looking for work that feels:
- warm but intelligent
- imaginative but structured
- bold in color, clear in silhouette, and strong in composition
- accessible for children without being visually disposable
- capable of handling both wonder and technical seriousness

The current visual system uses four arc colors:
- Galactic Playground: #7b2fff
- Mission to Mars: #ff6b35
- Silicon Astronaut: #00c9ff
- Automating the Galaxy: #9be564

Final candidates will receive the HexaVerse art direction package, character briefs, and production workflow.

---

## Initial Scope

### Book 1 Pilot Scope
- 1 cover illustration
- 12 interior illustrations minimum
- Optional chapter-break illustrations depending on final layout
- Participation in character style development before final production begins

### Likely Follow-On Scope
- Books 2-5 in the Galactic Playground arc
- Long-term continuation into Mission to Mars and later arcs if the fit is strong

---

## Deliverables

Per title, illustrators should expect to provide:
- High-resolution TIFF exports at 300 DPI
- Layered source files in PSD or equivalent editable format
- Print-ready PDF exports as needed
- Web-ready optimized image exports
- Alt text and scene notes to support accessibility review

---

## Working Process

Our workflow follows a structured sequence:
1. Character and style development
2. Pencil roughs
3. Line art approval
4. Color comps
5. Final rendering
6. Technical QA and delivery

Standard scope includes two revision rounds per illustration.

---

## Budget and Timeline

### Budget Guidance
- Cover illustration: $1,000-$2,500

- Expected total per book: $3,500-$7,500 depending on experience, complexity, and scope

### Timeline Guidance
- Approximate production window per book: 12-16 weeks
- Faster timelines may be possible after style calibration on the first title
