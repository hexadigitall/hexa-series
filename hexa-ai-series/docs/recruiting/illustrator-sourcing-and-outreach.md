# Illustrator Sourcing and Outreach Plan

**Purpose:** Practical sourcing channels, outreach templates, and screening flow for hiring a lead illustrator for HexaVerse.

---

## Hiring Goal

Hire one lead illustrator who can:
- establish the visual identity for Hexa, recurring characters, and the first arc
- execute Book 1 at production quality
- scale into Books 2-5 if the fit is strong
- help create repeatable visual rules that future illustrators can follow if the team expands

---

## Recommended Hiring Funnel

1. Build a longlist of 25-40 candidates.
2. Screen portfolios down to 8-12 serious candidates.
3. Interview 4-6 candidates.
4. Run a paid style test for 2-3 finalists.
5. Select 1 lead illustrator.
6. Keep 1-2 strong alternates for overflow or later arcs.

---

### Search filters to use
Search combinations such as:
- children's book illustrator STEM
- educational illustrator science kids
- recurring character illustrator picture book
- editorial illustrator space science
- inclusive children’s illustrator

---

## Portfolio Screening Rules

Advance only candidates who show at least three of the following clearly:
- strong recurring character work
- scene-based storytelling, not only character portraits
- confident use of color and composition
- work that fits ages 5-12 or can stretch there credibly
- evidence of educational, explanatory, or editorial clarity
- reliable craft across multiple pieces, not just one standout image

Reject quickly if you see:
- generic vector corporate style
- flat stock-like educational art with no personality
- style inconsistency across adjacent samples
- weak facial expressions or stiff poses
- no evidence of sequential or narrative thinking

---

## Suggested Paid Style Test

### Goal
Test whether the illustrator can translate HexaVerse direction into actual project-ready work before awarding the full Book 1 scope.

### Deliverables
Ask finalists to create:
- 1 black-and-white character exploration sheet for Hexa age 8
- 1 rough scene composition for a gravity moment from Why Does the Apple Fall?
- 1 simple color study using the Galactic Playground palette

### Timebox
- 4-8 hours expected effort

### Compensation
- Paid flat fee
- Recommended range: $200-$500 depending on scope and market

### Evaluation criteria
- Character readability and personality
- Fit with HexaVerse tone
- Ability to make science visible
- Composition and clarity
- Response to brief and communication quality

## Recommended Decision Standard

Do not hire purely on style. Choose the candidate who best combines:
- visual fit
- reliability
- structured workflow discipline
- clarity in educational storytelling
- ability to sustain quality across a series

A slightly less flashy but more consistent illustrator is often the better lead for a long-form educational series.
