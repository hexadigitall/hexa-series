# Illustrator Paid Style Test Brief

**Project:** HexaVerse lead illustrator evaluation

**Title:** Paid style test for Book 1 visual fit

**Compensation guidance:** $200-$500 flat fee

**Expected effort:** 4-8 hours

**Submission window:** 5 business days from brief receipt unless otherwise agreed

---

## Purpose

This paid style test is designed to answer one question: can you translate the HexaVerse visual direction into project-ready character and scene work for the Galactic Playground arc?

We are not asking for polished final art. We are evaluating interpretation, clarity, character thinking, and series fit.

---

## Materials Provided To Candidate

Share the following before the test begins:
- Art Direction Guide
- Character Design Briefs
- Illustrator Onboarding Package
- Book 1 manuscript: Why Does the Apple Fall?
- Any additional reference notes relevant to the selected scene

---

## Deliverables

Submit the following three items.

### 1. Hexa Character Exploration Sheet
Create one black-and-white exploration sheet for Hexa at age 8 in the Galactic Playground arc.

Include:
- one full-body front view
- one three-quarter or side pose
- three facial expressions: curious, delighted, determined
- one quick clothing/accessory exploration

Goal:
- show whether Hexa feels distinctive, warm, intelligent, and consistent with the brief

---

### 2. Scene Rough: Gravity Moment
Create one rough composition for a scene from Why Does the Apple Fall?

Recommended scene:
- Hexa observing an apple falling while trying to understand gravity

The rough should show:
- Hexa as the focal point
- a visible gravity teaching cue such as directional motion, arrows, staging, or visual emphasis
- enough environment to establish tone and context
- clear scene hierarchy for a child reader

Goal:
- test whether the candidate can make a science concept legible without becoming diagram-only

---

### 3. Color Study
Create one simple color study for the same scene using the Galactic Playground palette.

Include:
- use of #7b2fff as the dominant arc accent
- one or two supporting colors from the current HexaVerse palette
- enough color logic to show mood, focus, and readability

Goal:
- verify palette control and fit with the HexaVerse visual system

---

## Submission Format

Candidates may submit:
- PDF presentation deck, or
- one folder containing JPG/PNG previews plus editable working files if they prefer

Minimum delivery expectations:
- clean and readable roughs
- labels on each deliverable
- short written notes explaining any design choices or open questions

---

## Evaluation Criteria

We will score submissions on:

| Category | Weight |
|---|---|
| Fit with HexaVerse tone and audience | High |
| Character readability and personality | High |
| Scene composition and storytelling clarity | High |
| Educational clarity of the gravity concept | High |
| Color judgment and palette fit | Medium |
| Responsiveness to the brief | High |
| Communication and professionalism | Medium |

---

## What We Are Looking For

Strong submissions will show:
- Hexa feels like a recurring protagonist, not a generic child character
- the scene is easy to read quickly
- the science cue is visible and understandable
- the work feels warm, thoughtful, and visually durable
- the illustrator makes good tradeoffs between charm and clarity

Weak submissions usually show:
- attractive style with unclear teaching value
- inconsistent character thinking across sketches
- weak child-reader focus or confusing composition
- overrendering instead of solving the brief

---

## Rights and Usage

The style test remains the candidate's work. Hexadigitall may use it internally for hiring evaluation only unless a separate written agreement says otherwise.

Payment is for evaluation time and does not transfer production rights.

---

## Candidate Questions

Candidates may ask brief clarification questions before starting. We prefer to answer questions early rather than review work built on the wrong assumption.

---

## Decision Path After Submission

After review, candidates will receive one of the following outcomes:
- advance to final discussion / offer stage
- hold for future batch
- decline

If multiple candidates are strong, we may keep one lead and one reserve candidate.
