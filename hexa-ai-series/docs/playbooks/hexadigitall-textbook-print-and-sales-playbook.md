# Hexadigitall Textbook Print And Sales Playbook

Version: 1.0  
Owner: Hexadigitall Publishing, Curriculum, and Growth Teams  
Last Updated: 2026-04-22

## Purpose

This playbook defines how Hexadigitall titles move from approved manuscript to printed product and revenue-generating distribution.

It standardizes:

- print preparation and manufacturing readiness
- pricing and packaging logic
- channel strategy across schools, families, and retail
- launch sequencing, sales operations, and feedback loops

## Scope

Applies to all printable Hexadigitall products:

- core books
- companion textbooks
- workbooks
- lab guides
- teacher editions

## Product Packaging Framework

Each title should exist as a product family, not a single SKU.

Core SKU options:

- Student Edition (print)
- Teacher Edition (print plus guide pages)
- Workbook Add-on
- Classroom Pack (bulk)
- Bundle (print + digital companion)

### Bundle archetypes

- Starter Bundle: one title plus worksheet pack
- Arc Bundle: multiple titles from the same arc
- Term Bundle: semester-aligned teaching set
- School Pilot Bundle: classroom set with onboarding session

## Print Readiness Checklist

A title cannot enter manufacturing until all checks pass.

Editorial and content checks:

- canonical book standard passed
- final proofreading passed
- front matter and back matter complete
- glossary verified

Design and prepress checks:

- trim size approved
- bleed and margins validated
- CMYK and grayscale checks complete
- image DPI validated
- barcode and ISBN placement approved

Legal and rights checks:

- image rights cleared
- citation and attribution complete
- trademark and brand usage verified

Operational checks:

- print quantity forecast approved
- selected print partner confirmed
- sample proof approved
- launch inventory reserved

## Print Specification Baseline

Use consistent defaults unless a title-specific exception is approved.

- interior: legible textbook-safe serif or humanist sans
- minimum body size: age-appropriate by audience band
- line length and leading: tuned for readability
- paper quality: selected by use case (classroom durability vs premium gift)
- binding: selected by expected handling intensity

## Manufacturing Workflow

1. Freeze manuscript and metadata.
2. Export print-ready files.
3. Run automated and manual preflight checks.
4. Request physical and digital proofs.
5. Resolve proof issues.
6. Approve production run.
7. Receive and audit first batch.
8. Release to channels.

### Risk controls

- never skip proof stage
- never combine major content edits with final prepress cycle
- always verify first shipment against purchase order

## Pricing and Unit Economics

Pricing must balance mission access and business sustainability.

Inputs:

- print cost per unit
- shipping and warehousing
- platform and transaction costs
- discounts by channel
- educator affordability targets

### Pricing model guardrails

- define floor price using landed cost plus minimum margin
- define target price by audience willingness and channel
- define promotional bands for launches and pilots
- protect margins on low-volume SKUs

### Recommended discount structure

- direct family sales: light promotional discount windows
- schools and districts: volume-tier discounts
- resellers: wholesale margins with minimum advertised price policy

## Channel Strategy

Primary channels:

- direct web storefront
- school and district outreach
- education partners and nonprofits
- selected marketplaces and independent retail

### Channel notes

Direct channel:

- best margin
- best customer insight
- requires strong fulfillment and support

Institutional channel:

- lower churn
- larger order sizes
- longer procurement cycle

Retail and marketplace:

- broader reach
- higher fee pressure
- tighter packaging and listing standards

## Sales Motion by Segment

### Families and independent learners

- narrative-led positioning
- parent confidence messaging
- clear age fit and outcomes

### Teachers and schools

- standards alignment summary
- classroom implementation guide
- pilot offer and measurable outcomes

### District and institutional buyers

- procurement-ready documents
- implementation timeline
- training and support commitments

## Launch Playbook

Use a staged launch to reduce risk and improve conversion.

Phase 1: Internal readiness

- product pages complete
- sample chapters available
- FAQ and support scripts ready

Phase 2: Pilot release

- small educator cohort
- collect classroom feedback
- resolve friction points

Phase 3: General release

- public launch campaign
- partner amplification
- conversion tracking and weekly optimization

## Sales Enablement Assets

Required assets per title:

- one-page product sheet
- educator implementation brief
- parent guide summary
- sample chapter PDF
- comparison chart against adjacent titles
- launch email sequence and social copy

## KPI Framework

Track these metrics monthly and per release wave.

Demand metrics:

- preorders
- lead-to-order conversion
- repeat purchase rate

Revenue metrics:

- gross revenue
- net revenue
- average order value
- channel margin

Education impact metrics:

- classroom adoption count
- completion rate for companion activities
- teacher satisfaction and learner feedback

Operational metrics:

- print defect rate
- fulfillment time
- return rate

## Forecasting and Inventory Control

- build conservative baseline forecast from pilot demand
- maintain reorder thresholds by SKU
- separate evergreen titles from seasonal pushes
- avoid dead stock by bundling slow movers

## Returns, Errata, and Quality Response

- define clear return windows per channel
- maintain errata log per edition
- publish correction policy and replacement rules
- feed recurring issues back into book canonical standard

## Annual Sales Calendar

Core windows:

- back-to-school planning cycle
- term start pushes
- holiday and gift window
- exam and project season bundles

Plan every launch around these cycles when possible.

## Cross-Sell and Expansion Strategy

- link each title to next recommended title
- pair books with labs and course modules
- bundle by arc and outcome, not only by theme
- use capstone pathways for advanced learners and institutions

## Team Operating Rhythm

Weekly:

- sales pipeline review
- channel performance review
- fulfillment and support issues

Monthly:

- margin and pricing review
- inventory and reorder review
- campaign and conversion review

Quarterly:

- catalog health review
- edition updates
- strategic channel expansion decisions

## Governance

Decision owners:

- Curriculum Lead: educational integrity
- Publishing Lead: print quality and release readiness
- Growth Lead: pricing, channels, and campaigns
- Operations Lead: inventory and fulfillment reliability

No title enters public sale without all four approval lanes complete.

## Quick Start Execution Plan

1. Select one foundational title for print pilot.
2. Produce Student Edition and Teacher Edition only.
3. Run one proof cycle and one micro pilot with educators.
4. Adjust price and packaging from pilot data.
5. Launch direct channel first, then institutional outreach.
6. Review KPIs after 30 and 90 days.
