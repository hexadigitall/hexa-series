# HexaVerse Character Design Briefs

**Hexadigitall Technologies — April 2026**

---

## Hexa: Complete Character Profile

### Overview

**Hexa** is the protagonist of the HexaVerse series. Hexa grows alongside learners, appearing at different ages across the four arcs. Hexa is designed to be inclusive, curious, and capable—a role model who explores, questions, and builds solutions.

**Key trait:** Hexa is pragmatic and thoughtful; Hexa doesn't rush into solutions but asks good questions and tests ideas.

---

### Hexa Across the Arcs

#### Arc 1: The Galactic Playground (Age 8)

**Physical Description:**
- Round, expressive face with large, curious eyes
- Natural hair texture, medium-length (ear-length or longer)
- Warm skin tone (artist can vary; multiple skin tones are welcome; consistency across books is key)
- Freckles or subtle facial texture for personality
- Small stature; proportions appropriate for age 8

**Clothing (Signature Style):**
- Practical, colorful clothing suggesting a curious, outdoor-oriented kid
- Example: Cargo jacket in purple or teal + comfortable pants + sneakers
- Alt example: Overalls or romper with tech-inspired accessories
- One consistent clothing item that appears across Books 1-5 (e.g., a specific jacket or backpack)
- Small tech accessory: wrist band with a glow effect, safety goggles, or pocket device

**Poses and Expressions:**
- Often shown mid-action: reaching, climbing, pointing, experimenting
- Expressions: curious, excited, thoughtful, slightly uncertain (learning)
- Posture: energetic, engaged; often bending toward something to observe it closely

**Character Arc within Books 1-5:**
- Book 1 (Why Does the Apple Fall?): Wonder and discovery
- Book 2 (Sound and the Singing Air): Deeper questioning
- Book 3 (Light is Always in a Hurry): Building theories
- Book 4 (The Gravity Lab): Testing ideas
- Book 5 (My Robot Friend): Teaching others

---

#### Arc 2: Mission to Mars (Age 12-13)

**Physical Description:**
- Same face structure as Arc 1, now with slightly more angular features
- Taller, more athletic build (age-appropriate)
- Hair still natural texture, possibly a different length or style (evolution)
- Skin tone consistent with Arc 1 representation
- Eyes still large and expressive, but with more confidence

**Clothing (Mission-Ready):**
- Practical mission suit or field gear (not militaristic, but purposeful)
- Example: Tech-enhanced jacket + flexible pants + boots
- Alt example: Suit-like garment with visible pockets and utility design
- Visible tech: enhanced wrist device (larger than Arc 1), tool belt, communication device
- Color-coordinated with orange (#ff6b35) accents or trim

**Poses and Expressions:**
- More confident and active: problem-solving, leading, building
- Expressions: determined, focused, strategic, occasionally frustrated
- Posture: standing tall, often gesturing toward solutions or explaining to others
- Shown climbing, exploring, piloting, or constructing

**Character Arc within Books 6-10:**
- Book 6 (The Goldilocks Problem): Realizing the complexity
- Book 7 (The Glass Astronaut): Understanding human limits
- Book 8 (Fire Against the Wind): Making tradeoffs
- Book 9 (Code the Habitat): Learning to delegate to AI
- Book 10 (The Digital Twin): Thinking in systems

---

#### Arc 3: The Silicon Astronaut (Age 16-17)

**Physical Description:**
- Adult-like proportions; confident presence
- Facial features matured from Arc 2; strong and purposeful
- Same skin tone and hair texture as previous arcs (grown-up style)
- Eyes intelligent, reflective
- Visible subtle technological integration (hint of neural interface, augmentation)

**Clothing (Advanced/Professional):**
- High-tech research or command outfit
- Example: Fitted lab coat or advanced suit with visible tech seams
- Alt example: Commander suit with shoulder insignia or neural interface visible
- Accessories: Advanced wrist device, neural interface indicator (can be subtle: temple piece, ear band)
- Color-coordinated with cyan (#00c9ff) accents

**Poses and Expressions:**
- Confident, purposeful, often in leadership or mentorship roles
- Expressions: thoughtful, resolved, teaching, sometimes wondering about ethical implications
- Posture: standing with presence, often reaching toward technology or new frontiers
- Shown leading teams, making decisions, or working alongside AI

**Character Arc within Books 11-14:**
- Book 11 (Rewriting the Code of Life): Confronting ethical questions
- Book 12 (Mind and Machine): Merging with technology thoughtfully
- Book 13 (Edge of the Galaxy): Autonomy and responsibility
- Book 14 (The Programmable Frontier): Synthesizing knowledge into vision

---

#### Arc 4: Automating the Galaxy (Age 18-22)

**Physical Description:**
- Fully adult; strong, accomplished appearance
- Features consistent with previous arcs; recognizable as same person
- Visible technological integration: neural interface, augmentations are visible but elegant
- Confident and composed

**Clothing (Professional/Visionary):**
- Advanced tactical or command suit with integrated tech
- Visible augmentations (neural interface, wrist device, possibly more)
- Professional and forward-looking
- Color-coordinated with green (#9be564) accents

**Poses and Expressions:**
- Leadership and vision-setting
- Expressions: focused, strategic, sometimes visionary/hopeful
- Shown orchestrating systems, mentoring others, or making large-scale decisions

---

### Design Rules for Hexa

1. **Consistency across books in same arc:** Same clothing signature, same hair style, recognizable at a glance
2. **Smooth aging:** Hexa in Book 5 (end of Arc 1) should look ready to age into Arc 2; no sudden jumps
3. **Diversity inclusion:** Hexa's appearance can vary (skin tone, hair texture, facial features can be drawn authentically), but core proportions and personality must remain consistent
4. **Always curious:** Even in difficult moments, Hexa's eyes should show engagement and intelligence
5. **Active, not passive:** Hexa is shown *doing* something, not sitting passively; Hexa is the agent of learning

## Secondary Characters

### Mentor Figures

#### Galactic Playground Mentor: "Professor Nova"

**Role:** Wise, patient scientist who explains concepts and encourages curiosity

**Physical Description:**
- Age: 45-55 years old
- Warm, approachable face with kind eyes
- Shoulder-length or practical hair (can be gray/silver)
- Visible experience lines, but not stern; graceful aging
- Skin tone: Choose a diverse option; can vary from cover to interior

**Clothing Signature:**
- Lab coat (white or off-white) with practical pockets
- Underneath: casual but neat (simple shirt, sweater, or vest)
- Footwear: comfortable shoes (sneakers, practical boots, or sensible flats)
- Always carries: clipboard, measurement tool, telescope, or notebook
- Optional: Safety goggles on forehead or around neck

**Accessories:**
- Glasses (frames add character)
- Simple watch or wrist communicator
- Optional pendant or meaningful item

**Poses and Expressions:**
- Often bent slightly forward, listening and engaging
- Expressions: patient, encouraging, slightly amused by Hexa's questions
- Gesturing to explain or point out details in the world
- Sometimes sitting at a lab bench or looking through a telescope

**Relationship to Hexa:**
- Mentor and guide; Hexa trusts and respects Nova
- Nova encourages questioning and independent thinking
- Appears in all Books 1-5

**Visual Consistency:**
- Same clothing color/style across all 5 books
- Same accessories and identifying marks
- Age slightly by the end of Arc 1 (more gray hair, subtle changes)

---

#### Mission to Mars Mentor: "Commander Chen"

**Role:** Experienced engineer and mission leader who helps Hexa understand systems and tradeoffs

**Physical Description:**
- Age: 50-60 years old
- Confident, capable expression
- Shorter, practical hair (sometimes with gray)
- Skin tone: Diverse; can be medium to deep
- Strong posture; weathered hands

**Clothing Signature:**
- Mission suit or flight suit (designed for function)
- Color: Gray, tan, or dark blue with orange accents (#ff6b35)
- Equipment belts with tools, communication device
- Footwear: tactical boots
- Always visible: command insignia or badge

**Accessories:**
- Advanced wrist device (mission-critical)
- Clipboard or data pad
- Optional: Headset or communication earpiece

**Poses and Expressions:**
- Standing with authority; often explaining systems or procedures
- Expressions: focused, pragmatic, occasionally showing the weight of responsibility
- Gesturing to show how systems work
- Sometimes shown in profile, looking at distant Mars horizon

**Relationship to Hexa:**
- Mentor who challenges Hexa to think bigger
- Shows respect for Hexa's ideas but teaches about constraints
- Appears in all Books 6-10

---

#### Silicon Astronaut Mentor: "Dr. Keene"

**Role:** Advanced researcher in AI and bioengineering who pushes ethical boundaries

**Physical Description:**
- Age: 45-55 years old
- Intelligent, forward-thinking expression
- Hair possibly with augmentations visible (subtle tech in hair, or short practical style)
- Skin tone: Diverse option
- Visible signs of technological integration (subtle neural interface, etc.)

**Clothing Signature:**
- Advanced research suit or high-tech jacket
- Color: Sleek, professional (white, gray, or black with cyan accents)
- Visible pockets with advanced tools
- Footwear: sleek, futuristic sneakers or boots

**Accessories:**
- Advanced wrist device or neural interface control
- Glasses or AR display
- Clipboard or holographic interface
- Optional: Visible neural interface (temple band, ear piece)

**Poses and Expressions:**
- Often engaged with technology; gesturing to holographic displays
- Expressions: brilliant but cautious; wondering about ethical implications
- Engaging directly with Hexa, asking challenging questions
- Shown problem-solving or teaching

**Relationship to Hexa:**
- Mentor who engages Hexa in ethical thinking
- Respects Hexa's growing autonomy
- Appears in all Books 11-14

### Secondary Characters (Recurring)

#### Character 1: "Maya" (Ages 8-13, appears Galactic Playground → Mission to Mars)

**Role:** Hexa's peer; curious, creative, good at seeing patterns

**Age progression:**
- Galactic Playground (Ages 5-9): Age 7-8; smaller, enthusiastic
- Mission to Mars (Ages 10-14): Age 11-12; taller, more strategic

**Physical Description:**
- Different appearance from Hexa (skin tone, hair type, facial features)
- Warm, expressive eyes
- Natural hair texture (can be styled differently across books)

**Clothing Signature:**
- Lighter, more colorful outfit than Hexa
- Signature item: Maybe a bright scarf, colorful backpack, or patterned jacket
- Evolves with age but remains recognizable

**Personality in appearance:**
- Energetic, optimistic
- Often shown smiling or in animated conversation
- Gestures broadly

---

#### Character 2: "Koji" (Ages 12+, appears Mission to Mars → Silicon Astronaut)

**Role:** Pragmatic, detail-oriented peer; Hexa's technical partner

**Age progression:**
- Mission to Mars (Ages 10-14): Age 13-14; athletic build
- Silicon Astronaut (Ages 15+): Age 18-19; strong presence

**Physical Description:**
- Different appearance from Hexa and Maya
- Alert, focused eyes
- Practical hair and appearance

**Clothing Signature:**
- Technical, practical clothing (utility-focused)
- Signature item: Tool vest, tech-heavy backpack, or mission pack
- Always has something to fix or build

**Personality in appearance:**
- Focused, serious
- Often shown problem-solving or building
- Confident posture

---

#### Character 3: "Zephyr" (AI/Robot companion, constant across all arcs)

**Role:** Hexa's technological companion; loyal, helpful, learning alongside Hexa

**Design (Arc-agnostic):**
- Distinctive, recognizable robot/AI character
- Design should evolve subtly across arcs but remain clearly "Zephyr"
- Can have humanoid or unique form (artist choice)

**Physical Characteristics:**
- Clearly technological; visible circuits, panels, or geometric forms
- Color: Consistent across books (example: silver with blue accents)
- Size: Varies by context (handheld, human-sized, larger)
- Expression: Can display emotion through lights, face, or body language

**Personality in appearance:**
- Helpful and present; often positioned near Hexa's shoulder or hand
- Expressive despite being non-human
- Growing in capability across books

**Consistency rule:** Zephyr should be instantly recognizable across all 14 books

---

### Ensemble Characters

Other characters appear occasionally in single books or small groups (classmates, mission crew, other explorers). Design them to be diverse, approachable, and authentic.

**Diversity guidelines:**
- Vary skin tones, hair textures, body types, and abilities
- Show characters of different ages, backgrounds, and perspectives
- Include characters with disabilities in natural contexts (not tokenized)
- Ensure gender representation is diverse and non-stereotypical

## Design Reference Board: What to Create

To brief an illustrator, you should prepare:

1. **Character Sheet for Hexa** (one per arc):
   - Full-body front view
   - Full-body side view
   - 3-4 close-up expressions
   - Details of clothing, accessories
   - Pose references (curious, excited, determined, uncertain)

2. **Character Sheets for Mentors** (one per mentor):
   - Full-body views
   - Close-up face and expressions
   - Clothing and accessory details
   - Characteristic poses

3. **Secondary Character Reference Boards:**
   - Quick silhouettes showing Maya, Koji, Zephyr, and key ensemble cast
   - Thumbnail size okay; for quick reference

4. **Color & Style Study:**
   - Palette swatches with hex codes
   - Line weight examples
   - Background style examples
   - Character proportion guides

5. **Scene Composition References:**
   - 3-5 example layouts showing composition, character placement, detail level
   - Notes on focal points and visual hierarchy
