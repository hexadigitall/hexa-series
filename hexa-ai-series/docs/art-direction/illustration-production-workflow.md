# Illustration Production Workflow

**Hexadigitall Technologies — April 2026**

---

## Overview

This document outlines the end-to-end process for producing illustrations for the HexaVerse series. It covers roles, timelines, revision processes, technical specs, and quality gates.

---

## Illustration Scope by Title

### Scope: Standard Book (Per Title)

| Deliverable | Quantity | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Cover illustration | 1 | Full-page, front cover |
| Interior illustrations | 12 | Minimum 1 per chapter; mix of full-page, half-page, and spot illustrations |
| Chapter break images | 4 | Optional, to mark chapter transitions |
| **Total per book** | **17** | Can vary by content; 12-15 core illustrations minimum |

### Production Timeline (Per Book)

| Phase | Duration | Deliverables |
|---|---|---|
| **Design Kickoff** | 1 week | Character briefs, style direction, 3-5 cover concepts |
| **Pencil Roughs** | 2-3 weeks | Pencil sketches for all 13 interior illustrations + 1 cover option |
| **Content Review** | 1 week | Client feedback on composition, accuracy, consistency |
| **Line Art** | 2-3 weeks | Inked/finalized line work for all illustrations |
| **Color Comp** | 1-2 weeks | Flat colors (no shading) for feedback |
| **Color Review** | 1 week | Client feedback on color choices, accessibility, mood |
| **Final Rendering** | 2-3 weeks | Shading, texture, effects, polish |
| **Technical QA** | 1 week | DPI check, file format verification, color mode conversion |
| **Final Delivery** | — | High-res files, print-ready PDFs, web versions |
| **Total Timeline** | **12-16 weeks** | ~3-4 months per book |

## Roles and Responsibilities

### Client/Curriculum Team
- **Provide:** Story manuscript, key concepts, learning objectives
- **Brief:** Illustrator on character design, world-building rules, style direction
- **Review:** Sketches, line art, color comps, and final illustrations
- **Approve:** Character designs, cover concepts, all major decisions
- **Deliver feedback:** Constructively and promptly (within 3-5 business days)

### Illustrator
- **Deliver:** High-quality illustrations meeting art direction and technical specs
- **Follow:** Character design rules, color guidelines, accessibility standards
- **Provide:** Layered source files (PSD/AI), high-res TIFFs, print-ready PDFs
- **Iterate:** Up to 2 revision rounds per illustration (included in scope)
- **Communicate:** Proactively if questions arise or timeline is at risk

### Project Manager
- **Coordinate:** Communication between client and illustrator
- **Track:** Timeline, deliverables, budget
- **Escalate:** Issues that block progress
- **Manage:** File handoff and version control

## Detailed Workflow Phases

### Phase 1: Design Kickoff (Week 1)

**Deliverables from Client:**
1. Complete manuscript (story.md from relevant book)
2. Art direction guide (already created)
3. Character design briefs (already created)
4. 3-5 scene descriptions highlighting key moments that *must* be illustrated
5. Visual references (mood boards, color inspiration, style examples)
6. Accessibility requirements and brand guidelines

**Deliverables from Illustrator:**
1. Confirmation of understanding (style test or small sketch)
2. Questions and clarifications on character, world, tone
3. Proposed timeline and resource plan

**Client Feedback:**
- Approve style approach before major production starts
- Confirm character interpretations
- Resolve any ambiguities

---

### Phase 2: Pencil Roughs (Weeks 2-4)

**Illustrator Deliverables:**
- 12-13 pencil sketch illustrations (one per chapter, or more strategically placed)
- 1 cover concept sketch (can be multiple cover options)
- Simple notes on composition, focal points, and scene details
- Digital or high-quality scans

**Sketches should show:**
- Correct scene composition (rule of thirds, depth)
- Character proportions and poses
- Major focal points (what learner's eye goes to first)
- Rough scene background elements
- Hexa and/or secondary character placement

**What sketches DON'T need:**
- Fine detail
- Color
- High polish
- Background texture

**Client Review (3-5 business days):**
- [ ] Does composition serve the learning objective?
- [ ] Are character poses appropriate and recognizable?
- [ ] Does the scene accurately represent the text?
- [ ] Are there safety or accessibility concerns?
- [ ] Do we want adjustments before line art proceeds?

**Feedback categories:**
1. **Approved:** Proceed to line art
2. **Minor revisions:** Adjust pose, composition, or details (illustrator makes change)
3. **Major revisions:** Redraw (counts as 1 of 2 revision rounds)

---

### Phase 3: Line Art (Weeks 5-7)

**Illustrator Deliverables:**
- Final inked/digitally finalized line work for all approved sketches
- Clean, bold outlines; varied line weight for depth
- High-quality scans (300 DPI) or clean digital export
- Consistency check: All characters and world elements should feel cohesive

**Quality checks:**
- [ ] Lines are clean and confident (not sketchy)
- [ ] Line weight varies to create depth
- [ ] Characters are recognizable and consistent
- [ ] Text (if any) is clear and readable
- [ ] Background detail supports learning (labels, annotations visible)

**Client Review (3-5 business days):**
- [ ] Consistency: Does Hexa look the same across illustrations?
- [ ] Clarity: Are important details visible?
- [ ] Accuracy: Does the scene match the text?
- [ ] Style: Does it match the art direction?

**Feedback:**
1. **Approved:** Proceed to color
2. **Minor adjustments:** Illustrator makes small changes
3. **Major issues:** Redraw (revision round 1 or 2)

---

### Phase 4: Color Comps (Weeks 8-9)

**Illustrator Deliverables:**
- Flat color for all illustrations (no shading, no texture; solid color fills)
- Should clearly show color choices: arc colors, character colors, background palette
- Accurate to final vision but not polished yet

**Purpose of this phase:**
- Catch color issues early (accessibility, mood, consistency)
- Verify character color signatures are distinct and readable
- Ensure arc color is used correctly (primary or dominant accent)
- Test background palette

**Client Review (3-5 business days):**
- [ ] Colors align with arc and visual identity
- [ ] Character colors are consistent across all illustrations
- [ ] Color choices support the mood/learning objective
- [ ] WCAG AA contrast is likely (preliminary check)
- [ ] Arc color (#7b2fff, #ff6b35, #00c9ff, or #9be564) is clearly present

**Feedback:**
1. **Approved:** Proceed to final rendering
2. **Color adjustments:** Change specific colors (e.g., make background darker, change character clothing)
3. **Consistency issue:** Adjust character or world colors to match other illustrations

---

### Phase 5: Final Rendering (Weeks 10-12)

**Illustrator Deliverables:**
- Fully rendered illustrations with:
  - Shading and lighting
  - Texture (clouds, metal, fabric, skin)
  - Effects (glows, shadows, depth)
  - Polish and refinement
- High-res TIFF (300 DPI, RGB)
- Layered PSD (for future edits)

**Quality expectations:**
- Professional, polished appearance
- Consistent style across all illustrations
- Readable at both large (book page) and small (thumbnail) sizes
- Accessible color choices (tested)
- Accurate details (no scientific errors)

**Client Final Review (5-7 business days):**
- [ ] Quality is professional and polished
- [ ] Style is consistent with art direction
- [ ] Characters are recognizable and well-rendered
- [ ] Color is vibrant, accessible, and on-brand
- [ ] No scientific or factual errors
- [ ] Supports learning objectives

**Feedback (Final revision round if needed):**
- Only critical issues at this stage
- Minor touch-ups by illustrator (included)
- Major revisions would use revision round 2

---

### Phase 6: Technical QA (Week 13)

**Before Print/Publication:**

**File verification:**
- [ ] High-res TIFF: 300 DPI at final print size (2550 x 3300 pixels for 8.5" x 11")
- [ ] File size: Under 50 MB per file (manageable)
- [ ] Color mode: RGB for digital; CMYK for print
- [ ] Bleed: 0.25" on all sides for print files
- [ ] Filename: Consistent naming (e.g., "book-01-chapter-01-gravity-basics.tif")

**Accessibility verification:**
- [ ] Alt text provided for all illustrations (100-150 characters, descriptive)
- [ ] Color contrast checked against WCAG AA standard (4.5:1 for text)
- [ ] No red/green combinations without additional differentiation

**Archival:**
- [ ] High-res TIFF backed up
- [ ] Layered PSD archived
- [ ] Web versions (72 DPI JPG) created
- [ ] Print-ready PDF created (CMYK with bleed)

## Revision and Feedback Protocol

### Revision Rounds (Included in Scope)

**Standard engagement includes 2 revision rounds per illustration.**

**Revision round 1:** Usually during pencil or line art phase
- Client feedback identifies significant changes
- Illustrator implements changes and resubmits
- Counts as "1 revision round"

**Revision round 2:** Usually during color or final phase
- Additional changes requested
- Illustrator implements and final version delivered
- Counts as "2 revision rounds"

**Additional revisions:** Billable at hourly rate or per-revision fee

### Feedback Guidelines

**For Client (giving feedback):**
1. Be specific: "The pose feels passive; can Hexa be reaching toward something?" (not "change it")
2. Explain why: "This needs to show active learning because the text says Hexa is experimenting"
3. Suggest alternatives: "Can we try this angle instead, or would this reference help?"
4. Acknowledge what's working: "Love the expression; the detail on the robot is perfect"
5. Respond within 3-5 business days (to keep timeline moving)

**For Illustrator (receiving feedback):**
1. Ask clarifying questions if feedback is ambiguous
2. Propose solutions: "I can adjust the pose like this, or try this reference if you prefer"
3. Communicate blockers early: "I'll need more reference material for this scene" or "This might push timeline by 2-3 days"
4. Deliver revisions within agreed timeframe
5. Keep an updated version control system (version 1, version 2, final, etc.)

## File Management and Handoff

### Naming Convention

All files should follow this pattern:

```
[book-slug]_[chapter-number]_[scene-description]_[version].[extension]

Example:
why-does-the-apple-fall_01_apple-falling_v1.psd
why-does-the-apple-fall_01_apple-falling_final.tif
why-does-the-apple-fall_cover_galactic-playground_v2.psd
```

### Deliverable Package (Per Book)

When production is complete, illustrator delivers:

1. **High-res TIFF files** (13 illustrations + 1 cover)
   - 300 DPI, RGB, no compression
   - File names per convention above
   - Includes bleed (0.25" on all sides)

2. **Layered Source Files (PSD/AI)**
   - One per illustration for editing/revisions
   - Organized layers (sketch, line art, base color, shading, effects, text)
   - Font files included (if applicable)

3. **Print-Ready PDF**
   - CMYK color mode
   - Bleed included
   - All fonts embedded
   - Intended for printing press

4. **Web Versions (JPG)**
   - 72 DPI
   - RGB color mode
   - Optimized file size (<500 KB per image)
   - For website/digital display

5. **Metadata Document**
   - Alt text for each illustration
   - Scene descriptions
   - Character notes (e.g., "This is Hexa age 8, excited about gravity")
   - Technical notes (fonts used, special effects, etc.)

## Quality Checklist (Final Delivery)

Before final payment/publication, verify:

### Art and Content
- [ ] All 13-14 illustrations delivered (or agreed-upon quantity)
- [ ] Cover illustration matches approved concept
- [ ] Hexa appears consistent across all illustrations
- [ ] Secondary characters are recognizable
- [ ] No scientific or factual errors
- [ ] Illustrations support learning objectives
- [ ] Tone matches arc (wonder for Galactic, systems for Mission, etc.)

### Technical
- [ ] All files at 300 DPI
- [ ] File names follow convention
- [ ] High-res TIFF in RGB
- [ ] Layered PSD with organized layers
- [ ] Print-ready PDF in CMYK
- [ ] Web versions at 72 DPI JPG
- [ ] All files under 50 MB (manageable)
- [ ] Bleed included on print files

### Brand and Accessibility
- [ ] Arc color (#7b2fff / #ff6b35 / #00c9ff / #9be564) is visible
- [ ] Alt text provided for all illustrations
- [ ] Color contrast passes WCAG AA (4.5:1)
- [ ] No red/green combinations without differentiation
- [ ] Typography uses Orbitron/Exo 2 fonts
- [ ] Diversity and representation are authentic

### Documentation
- [ ] Metadata document complete (alt text, scene descriptions)
- [ ] Character reference notes included
- [ ] Technical notes (fonts, effects, etc.)
- [ ] Revision history documented

## Cost and Budget Guidance

### Typical Illustration Costs (Per Book)

| Scope | Cost Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 13 interior illustrations | $2,500–$5,000 | Depends on artist level and complexity |
| 1 cover illustration | $1,000–$2,500 | Often premium; represents the book |
| **Total per book** | **$3,500–$7,500** | Professional, experienced illustrator |

**Budget variables:**
- **Artist experience:** Emerging vs. established children's book illustrator
- **Complexity:** Detailed backgrounds vs. simple; technical subject matter
- **Revisions:** Included rounds (2) vs. additional
- **Rush fee:** Expedited timeline (+25-50%)
- **Licensing:** Exclusive vs. non-exclusive (affects cost)

### Payment Structure (Recommended)

1. **Upon contract signing:** 25% deposit
2. **Upon pencil approval:** 25%
3. **Upon line art approval:** 25%
4. **Upon final delivery:** 25%

Or split as:
- 50% upon contract signing
- 50% upon final delivery

## Timeline for Multi-Book Production

To scale illustration efficiently, consider:

**Batch approach:**
- **Batch 1 (Books 1-3):** Start with illustrator learning the style; expect slightly longer timeline
- **Batch 2 (Books 4-5):** Illustrator is faster; shorter timeline
- **Batch 3 (Books 6-8):** New illustrator or same; continue scaling
- **Parallel tracks:** If budget allows, run 2 illustrators in parallel (one on Books 1-7, one on Books 8-14)

**Total project timeline:**
- 1 illustrator, 14 books: ~24 months (2 years)
- 2 illustrators, 14 books in parallel: ~12-14 months (1 year)
